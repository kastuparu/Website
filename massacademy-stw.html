Science and Technical Writing

Science and Technical Writing (STW) is taught by Dr. Crowthers. He also teaches STEM I and STEM II, which are to help us develop our STEM projects. STW is a class where we work on developing the skills needed for STEM projects, such as learning how to effectively research, take notes, and produce a piece of writing.

In November, I created a Project Proposal for my STEM I project which includes all the background information necessary for regional and state approval before starting testing. My project notes document was where I took notes on all the research I did for my project, helping me keep organized for my literature review. The literature review explains all the background information that is necessary for my project. In January, we researched a statistical test to present for the class so that we could learn about statistics to use in our STEM I projects. My group members (Rachel and Daiwik) and I learned about Spearman's Rank Correlation test, used to show the relationship between two monotonic variables.
