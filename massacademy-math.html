Mathematics

Mass Academy’s math class, taught by Mr. Regele, is all about developing problem-solving skills and learning about mathematical modeling. In class, we worked individually or in groups to improve our problem solving skills. Throughout the year, we did problems from Exeter’s Book 2 and 3 as well as PCMI PROMYS to become familiar with mathematical concepts needed for calculus.

Triangle of Evens and Odds

On the first day of school, the problem that we worked on in class was proving that every row after the second row in a triangle of numbers contains an entry which is even. The triangle is formed by summing the cell above it and the two cells immediately to the left and right of the cell above it (except the top which is 1).

HiMCM

In November, we competed in the HiMCM (High School Mathematical Contest in Modeling), which is a 36-hour math modeling competition. The final product is a paper written to explain the modeling solution. My team members (Ronit and Nick) and I decided to choose the first problem because although we anticipated that the mathematical analysis was more complex, the second problem required more data organization. We thought our team of three (most teams had four) would have a higher chance of success with the problem focused around mathematical analysis. My team had a lot of fun with the HiMCM and is proud of our solution!

Conic Art

The conic art assignment was an especially fun one, in which we got to draw anything of our choice using parabolas, ellipses, hyperbolas, and sinusoidal functions with no straight lines allowed. I chose to draw the Among Us emergency meeting logo. My design relied the most on ellipses and parabolas.

Painted Tetrahedron

The painted tetrahedron was one of the final problems we worked on during the school year. The question posed a situation where a large tetrahedron was dipped in paint and then cut into unit tetrahedra. In the end, I found out the number of smaller tetrahedra and octahedra with 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4 sides covered in paint based on side length n of the large tetrahedron.
